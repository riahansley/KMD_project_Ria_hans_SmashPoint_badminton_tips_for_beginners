Start Strong. Play Smart. Smash Better.

Simple badminton tips for beginners to build skills, confidence, and love for the game from day one.

Players and Match Types

  • Singles: one player on each side
  • Doubles: two players on each side

Scoring System

  • Matches are played using the rally scoring system.
  • A game is won by the first side to score 21 points.
  • A point is awarded on every rally, regardless of which side served.
  • A game must be won by a minimum margin of two points.
  • If the score reaches 29–29, the side that scores the 30th point wins the game.
  • Matches are typically played as best of three games.

Serving Rules

  • The serve must be executed underhand.
  • At the moment of contact, the shuttle must be struck below the server’s waist.
  • The shuttle must be served diagonally into the opponent’s service court.
  • Both feet of the server must remain in contact with the court surface during the serve.

Service Court Position

  • When the server’s score is even, the serve is delivered from the right service court.
  • When the server’s score is odd, the serve is delivered from the left service court.
  • In doubles, the serving side continues to serve until a rally is lost, after which service passes to the opposing team.

In and Out Rules

  • A shuttle landing on the boundary line is considered in.
  • A shuttle landing outside the boundary lines is considered out.

Faults | A fault is called if:

  • The shuttle is hit above the server’s waist during a serve.
  • The shuttle fails to land in the correct service court.
  • A player touches the net with their racket or body.
  • A player hits the shuttle twice in succession.
  • The shuttle is carried or thrown instead of cleanly struck.
  • A player obstructs or distracts an opponent.

Let | A let maybe called to:

  • replay a rally in the event of an interruption or unforeseen disturbance.
